The NA miata was built from 1990-1997. It is the first generation. It weighs approximately 2,250lbs. It was available with a removeable hardtop. The 1990-1993 Miatas came with 1.6L 116hp engines. The 1994-1997 Miatas had a 1.8L 132hp engine. The NA's most defining feature is its pop-up headlights. Pictured above is my 1993 Limited Edition #34. The NA Miata represented the first new roadster introduced into the US in over 10 years.

The NB miata was built from 1999-2005. It is the second generation. It weighs approximately 2,300lbs. It came with an updated 1.8L engine with solid lifter instead of hydraulic lifters. The engine was rated at 140hp and was updated to 142hp in 2001 with the introductino of VVT. The same removeable hard-top from the NA generation fits the NB model. Pictured above is my 2000 Miata fitted with a removable hardtop.

The NC miata was built from 2006-2015. It is the third generation and it weighs approximately 2,500lbs. This generation is the largest and heaviest Miata and is said to be the best grand tourer of the lineage. The car has a 2.0L engine rated at 160hp. Pictured above is Flyin' Miata's "Atomic Betty," an LS V8 swapped conversion with more than 400hp. The NC was available with a removable hardtop or a power retractable hardtop.

The ND Miata is the current generation and was introduced for the 2016 model year. It weighs approximately 2,300lbs. It is a radical departure from the NC because it is significantly lighter and smaller. The ND is actually slightly shorter than even the NA in length. Pictured above is a Miata RF, a kind of power targa top. The ND was originally released with a 2.0L 155hp engine, but the engine was updated to 181hp for the 2019 model year.
